2. Information we collect

eirGate may collect and process the following information:

  • Account and user information: name, email address where provided, mobile phone number, user role and permissions, company or gate assignment, and login credentials or authentication data.
  • Gate access and event information: gate open commands, auxiliary relay commands, call-to-open attempts, access allowed or denied records, and the date, time, user and gate identity linked to access events.
  • App and device security information: app install identifier, app device identifier, phone-binding status where enabled, login/session information, and security/audit events.
  • Gate hardware information: gate device ID, SIM ICCID, modem IMEI, signal strength/RSSI, online/offline heartbeat status, MQTT connection status, GNSS location of the fixed gate device, input states, relay events, and ATECC608C secure module serial/status where fitted.

eirGate does not use the mobile app to track a user’s live personal location. Any GNSS location stored by eirGate relates to the fixed gate hardware module, not the phone user.

3. Why we use this information

eirGate uses this information to:

  • Authenticate users and manage authorised access.
  • Allow authorised users to open assigned gates.
  • Manage companies, gates, roles and permissions.
  • Record access and relay events for audit and security purposes.
  • Support call-to-open using recognised caller phone numbers.
  • Show gate online/offline, signal, input and diagnostic status.
  • Detect faults and support escalation where configured.
  • Improve security and prevent unauthorised access.
  • Provide technical support and comply with legal, billing, security and operational requirements.

4. Call-to-open and telephony

Where call-to-open is enabled, a user may call an eirGate-assigned telephone number. eirGate receives the caller phone number, checks whether that phone number is authorised for the gate, triggers the configured relay if allowed, and rejects the call. The caller number and call result may be logged for security and audit purposes.

5. Phone binding

Phone binding may be disabled, monitored, or enforced by an administrator. When enabled, eirGate may link a user account to an app installation or device identifier to reduce unauthorised use of credentials on another phone.

6. Secure module authentication

Where supported hardware is installed, eirGate may record secure module details such as the ATECC608C serial number, module status and verification events. This is used to help confirm that a gate module is genuine and authorised.

7. Sharing of information

eirGate does not sell user data. eirGate may use trusted service providers to operate the service, including hosting, database, telephony, payment, email or support providers. These providers process information only as needed to provide the eirGate service.

Examples may include cloud hosting providers, database providers, Twilio for call-to-open, Stripe for billing/payment where used, and email/support providers where used.

eirGate may also disclose information where required by law, regulation, court order, security investigation, or to protect the rights and safety of users, companies or property.

8. Security

eirGate uses encrypted connections where available, including HTTPS/TLS between the app and backend services. Access to administrative functions is restricted by user role. Security logs may be retained to investigate misuse, faults or unauthorised access.

9. Data retention

eirGate keeps account, access and diagnostic records for as long as needed to provide the service, maintain security, support customers, comply with legal requirements and resolve disputes. Retention periods may vary depending on company policy, contract terms, security needs and legal obligations.
